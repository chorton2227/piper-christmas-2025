## ğŸ‘¨â€ğŸ‘©â€ğŸ‘§ How to Help

### Before They Start

**1. Help with Planning (30-60 minutes)**
- Review the project ideas document together
- Discuss what topic excites them most
- Help them complete the planning worksheet
- Ensure they understand all requirements
- Approve their plan before they start coding

**2. Content Gathering**
- Help collect or create images (ensure appropriate content)
- Assist with finding external links (verify they're safe/appropriate)
- Proofread their written content
- Help organize information into logical sections

### During the Project

**3. Provide Structure & Support**
- Encourage them to build one page at a time
- Remind them to save frequently
- Suggest regular breaks (every 30-45 minutes)
- Help them test pages in the browser
- Be available for questions but let them problem-solve

**4. Common Challenges & Solutions**

**"I don't know what to write about!"**
- Review the 13 project ideas provided
- Suggest combining interests (gaming + learning, pets + care guides)
- Remind them it can be simpleâ€”what they know well

**"My links aren't working!"**
- Check that filenames match exactly (case-sensitive)
- Verify files are in the same folder
- Make sure `.html` extension is included

**"Images won't show up!"**
- Verify image files are in the `images` folder
- Check filename spelling and extension
- Ensure path is correct: `images/filename.jpg`

**"This is taking too long!"**
- Break it into smaller tasks
- Focus on completing one section at a time
- Remind them quality over speed
- It's okay to work on it over multiple days

### After Completion

**5. Review & Feedback**
- Look through the completed site together
- Use the checklist to verify all requirements
- Test all links and images
- Provide positive, constructive feedback
- Celebrate their accomplishment!

**6. Share Their Work**
- Show it to family members
- Let them demonstrate what they've built
- Take screenshots to save progress
- Prepare for Module 2 (CSS) to make it look amazing!
